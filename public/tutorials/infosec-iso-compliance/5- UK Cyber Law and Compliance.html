Computer Misuse Act 1990 — Overview
Five offence tiers address unauthorised access through to acts endangering national security. The Serious Crime Act 2015 introduced S.3ZA (life imprisonment) and S.3A (articles for use in offences). Sentences have risen substantially since the original 1990 Act.

DPA 2018 Offences — Financial Penalties Only
DPA 2018 criminal offences (S.170, S.171, S.173) carry no custodial sentences — courts impose financial penalties only, though amounts are unlimited on conviction on indictment. The ICO also has a civil enforcement tier for organisations (up to £17.5m or 4% global turnover under UK GDPR).

Why IAM = Legal Compliance
The CMA turns on whether access was "authorised" — IAM controls define and enforce that boundary. The DPA 2018 S.170 offence of unlawfully obtaining personal data is mitigated by IAM limiting who can access what. Both frameworks reward organisations with mature IAM when assessing culpability and proportionality of penalties.
Data (Use and Access) Act 2025

DUAA 2025 — Key Changes

Amends but does not replace UK GDPR, DPA 2018, and PECR. Phased implementation June 2025 — June 2026.

📋
What the DUAA Does and Does Not Do
The DUAA amends the existing UK data protection framework — it does not create a standalone new regime. UK GDPR and the DPA 2018 remain in force. Many provisions require secondary legislation or ICO guidance before they take full effect, and as of mid-2025 significant supporting guidance remains outstanding, giving some organisations limited preparation time.

☁️
Cloud Raises Every Legal Issue Simultaneously
Moving to the cloud does not reduce legal obligations — it multiplies them. Data residency triggers GDPR jurisdiction issues. Shared infrastructure raises CMA authorisation questions. Third-party processors require Article 28 contracts. Cookies and analytics remain subject to PECR regardless of cloud architecture.
