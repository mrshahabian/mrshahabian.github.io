abstract: "This work investigates vision–language models (VLMs) for fall detection in socially assistive robotics, comparing zero-shot prompting against few-shot calibration. We study how prompt design and a small number of calibration examples affect detection reliability relative to skeleton-based supervised baselines, with attention to deployment in real assisted-living environments. The study contributes practical guidance for using foundation models in safety-relevant human-monitoring tasks."

Accepted at the 18th International Conference on Social Robotics + Art (ICSR 2026), London, UK. Published by Springer in the Lecture Notes in Artificial Intelligence (LNAI) series.

This paper examines how vision–language foundation models can be applied to fall detection — a safety-critical task in assistive care — using zero-shot prompting and lightweight few-shot calibration rather than full supervised training.
